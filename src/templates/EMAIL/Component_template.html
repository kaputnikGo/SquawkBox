How to fulfil your privacy obligations to non–employees

Dear Reader,
In last Wednesday’s Health & Safety Bulletin, we looked at the Australian Privacy Principles legislated in the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth). As promised, today Charles Power will look at some of the APPs that relate to non–employees’ personal information in more detail.

This is often relevant, because the personal information of non–employees does not fall under the employee records exemption, which may exempt you from the APPs when you are dealing with records that directly relate to a current or former employment relationship.

When it comes to the personal information of non–employees, the APPs require you to take certain steps in relation to:

•

your organisation’s privacy policy;

•

dealing with unsolicited job applications; and

•

transferring personal information offshore.

Read on for the details.
Until next time,

Non–employees’ personal information: Your APP obligations

 

Dear Reader, In last Wednesday’s Health & Safety Bulletin, we looked at the Australian Privacy Principles legislated in the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth). As promised, today Charles Power will look at some of the APPs that relate to non–employees’ personal information in more detail.

Your privacy policy
APP 1 requires an organisation to:

•

  

take reasonable steps to implement practices, procedures and systems to:
   
 

–

ensure compliance with APPs and any binding registered APP code, i.e. a set of privacy principles approved by the Privacy Commissioner for a particular organisation; and

  
   
 

–

deal with related inquiries and complaints;

•

  

have a clearly expressed and up–to–date privacy policy about how personal information is managed; and

•

  

take reasonable steps to make its privacy policy available
   
   

–

free of charge;
   
 

–

in an appropriate form (e.g. online); and
   
 

–

on request and in a particular form, as requested.
     

Unsolicited job applications

APP 4 outlines the steps you must take if your organisation receives unsolicited personal information, i.e. personal information that you did not take any active steps to collect.

For example, if you receive an employment application from a job seeker, which is not in response to an advertised vacancy, you must satisfy yourself that the information is reasonably necessary for, or directly related to, one or more of your organisation’s functions or activities.
